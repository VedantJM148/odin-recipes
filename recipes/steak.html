Description

Steak is a timeless dish loved for its rich, savory flavor and satisfying juiciness. Whether grilled, pan-seared, or broiled, a perfectly cooked steak can elevate any meal.

This recipe focuses on pan-searing a ribeye with garlic butter for maximum tenderness and flavor. It’s simple, classic, and brings out the best in high-quality beef.

Ingredients

  • 2 ribeye steaks (about 1-inch thick)
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 3 cloves garlic, smashed
  • 2 tablespoons unsalted butter
  • 2 sprigs fresh rosemary (or thyme)
  • Salt and freshly ground black pepper, to taste

Steps

  1. Remove steaks from the fridge 30 minutes before cooking to bring to room temperature.
  2. Season generously with salt and pepper on both sides.
  3. Heat olive oil in a cast iron skillet over high heat until just smoking.
  4. Sear steaks for 3–4 minutes per side for medium-rare, adjusting time for desired doneness.
  5. Add butter, garlic, and rosemary to the pan. Tilt the skillet and spoon melted butter over the steaks.
  6. Remove from heat, rest steaks for 5 minutes, then serve.
